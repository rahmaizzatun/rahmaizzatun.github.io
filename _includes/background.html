Background

I’m a fresh Accounting graduate from Jenderal Soedirman University with a deep curiosity for how numbers reflect reality and how audits help keep that reality honest. During my internship at a public accounting firm, I took part in external audits across different industries, assisting in stock opname, examining financial statements, and tracing evidence to its source.

It was in those moments of comparing figures to facts and confirming the smallest details that I discovered how auditing is both an art and a responsibility. I came to appreciate that every audit tells a story not just about numbers but about integrity, risk, and trust.

Outside of academics, I enjoy meeting people from different countries and exchanging ideas. I am currently volunteering at the International Relations Office as a BIPA Assistant, where I serve as a cultural buddy. In this role, I help international students adapt to the local environment and learn Bahasa Indonesia. I believe that staying curious and open-minded is essential for both personal and professional growth.
